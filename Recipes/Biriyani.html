Biriyani

Description

Biriyani is a fragrant and flavorful mixed rice dish. It's made with spices, rice, and meat (chicken, mutton, beef) or vegetables.

Ingredients

Biriyani (Spicy Rice with Chicken/Vegetables)
  • For Marination (for Chicken Biriyani)
    • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
    • 1/2 cup plain yogurt (dahi)
    • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder (haldi)
    • 1/2 tsp garam masala
    • 1 tsp lemon juice
    • Salt to taste
  • For Rice and Base
    • 2 cups basmati rice
    • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
    • 1/2 cup ghee or oil
    • 1 tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
    • Whole spices: 2-3 green cardamom, 2-3 cloves, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 1 bay leaf
    • 2-3 green chilies, slit
    • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
    • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
    • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
    • 1/2 cup fried onions (for garnish)
  • For Spice Mix
    • 1 tsp red chili powder
    • 1 tsp biriyani masala powder
    • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder (haldi)
    • 1 tsp coriander powder (dhaniya)
    • Salt as required

Steps

  1. For Biriyani
    • Prepare Chicken: Marinate the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and lemon juice. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
    • Cook the Rice: Wash the basmati rice and soak for 30 minutes. Boil a large pot of water with some salt and whole spices. Add the soaked rice and cook until it's about 70-80% done. Drain the rice and set aside.
    • Make the Biriyani Base: Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or pot. Add whole spices and cumin seeds. Let them splutter. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and saut√© for a minute.
    • Add the Marinated Chicken: Add the marinated chicken and stir well. Cook on high heat for 5-7 minutes until the chicken is slightly cooked. Add the powdered spices, mint, and coriander leaves. Cook until the oil separates.
    • Layering (Dum Cooking): This is the key step. Spread a layer of partially cooked rice over the chicken. Sprinkle some fried onions, mint, and coriander leaves. Add another layer of rice and repeat. You can also drizzle some saffron milk (saffron soaked in warm milk) for color and flavor.
    • Dum Cooking: Cover the pot tightly with a lid. You can seal the edges with dough to trap the steam. Cook on very low heat for 20-25 minutes. This process allows the flavors to infuse.
    • Serve: Gently mix the biriyani from the sides and serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or salad.
