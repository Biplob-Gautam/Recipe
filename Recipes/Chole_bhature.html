Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (Chole) and deep-fried leavened flatbread (Bhature).

Ingrediants

Chole (Spicy Chickpea Curry)
  • 2 cups dried white chichpea (kabuli chana) soaked overnight
  • Whole spices (optional) -> 1-inch cinnamon, 2 green cardamom, 2-3 cloves, 1 bay-leaf
  • For Gravy
    • 3 Tbps ghee
    • i tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
    • i large onion, finely chopped or pureed
    • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder (haldi)
    • 2 tsp coriander powder (dhaniya)
    • 1-2 tsp red chilli powder
    • 1 tsp Chole Masala powder
    • 1 tsp dried mango powder (amchur)
    • salt as required
    • fresh coriander leaves chopped
    • 2-3 green chillies, slit
  • For Bhature
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour (Maida)
    • 1/2 cup semolina (sooji)
    • 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 1/2 cup plain yoghurt (dahi)
    • 2 Tbsp oil or ghee
    • water as needed, lukewarm
    • oil for deep-frying

Steps

  1. For Chole
    • Boil Chickpeas : Drain the soaked chickpeas and rinse. Add them to a pressure cooker with fresh water, salt, baking soda, tea bags/spice bundle, and whole spices (if using). Add enough water to cover the chickpeas by about an inch. Pressure cook for 4-5 whistles or until the chickpeas are very tender. Discard the tea bag/spice bundle and save the cooking liquid.
    • Prepare the Masala : Heat oil in a pan. Add the whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon) and let them sizzle. Add the asafoetida. Then add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
    • Add the spices : Add the tomato puree and all the powdered spices (turmeric, chili, coriander, cumin, chole masala, and salt). Cook the mixture until the oil separates from the masala.
    • Combine and simmer : Add the boiled chickpeas (with the cooking water) to the masala. Stir well, cover, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes on low heat. This allows the flavors to meld. Garnish with fresh coriander.
  2. For Bhature
    • Repare the dough : In a large bowl, mix the flour, semolina, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
    • Knead the dough : Add the yogurt and mix. Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a soft, smooth dough. The dough should be slightly sticky. Add a little oil and knead again until the stickiness is gone.
    • Rest the dough : Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest in a warm place for at least 2 hours. This is crucial for the bhature to become fluffy.
    • Roll and fry : Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls. Roll each ball into an oval or round shape about 4-5 inches in diameter.
      • Deep-fry : Heat oil in a deep pan or wok on high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide in a rolled bhatura. Gently press it down with a spatula. It will puff up. Flip it and fry the other side until it's golden brown.
    • Serve hot : Remove the bhatura from the oil and serve immediately with the hot chole.
