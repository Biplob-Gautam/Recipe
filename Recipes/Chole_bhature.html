Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (Chole) and deep-fried leavened flatbread (Bhature).

Ingrediants

Chole (Spicy Chickpea Curry)
  • 2 cups dried white chichpea (kabuli chana) soaked overnight
  • Whole spices (optional) -> 1-inch cinnamon, 2 green cardamom, 2-3 cloves, 1 bay-leaf
  • For Gravy
    • 3 Tbps ghee
    • i tsp cumin seeds (jeera)
    • i large onion, finely chopped or pureed
    • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
    • 1 tsp turmeric powder (haldi)
    • 2 tsp coriander powder (dhaniya)
    • 1-2 tsp red chilli powder
    • 1 tsp Chole Masala powder
    • 1 tsp dried mango powder (amchur)
    • salt as required
    • fresh coriander leaves chopped
    • 2-3 green chillies, slit
  • For Bhature
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour (Maida)
    • 1/2 cup semolina (sooji)
    • 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 1 tsp sugar
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 1/2 cup plain yoghurt (dahi)
    • 2 Tbsp oil or ghee
    • water as needed, lukewarm
    • oil for deep-frying

Steps

  1. For Chole
    • Boil Chickpeas : Drain the soaked chickpeas and rinse. Add them to a pressure cooker with fresh water, salt, baking soda, tea bags/spice bundle, and whole spices (if using). Add enough water to cover the chickpeas by about an inch. Pressure cook for 4-5 whistles or until the chickpeas are very tender. Discard the tea bag/spice bundle and save the cooking liquid.
    • Prepare Gravy Base : Heat oil/ghee in a large pan or kadai. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
